# *The Nation We Forgot to Build*

*“India is not poor because it lacks resources. It is kept poor because poverty is politically profitable. The potential is staggering. The mismanagement is deliberate.”*

There is a conversation happening across India, in tea stalls and WhatsApp groups, in college corridors and office canteens, a conversation that rarely makes it to prime time television precisely because it is too honest, too uncomfortable, and too dangerous for those who hold power. It is a conversation about the enormous gap between what India is and what India could be. This article is that conversation, written down.

India in 2026 sits at a historic crossroads. It is the world’s most populous nation, the fastest-growing major economy, home to the largest youth demographic in human history and simultaneously one of the most squandered civilizational potentials on the planet. The raw material for greatness exists in abundance. What is missing is the leadership, the accountability, and the patriotic honesty to use it.

What follows is not a partisan attack on any single party. It is a reckoning with a political culture; one that spans every party, every ideology, every generation of leadership since independence, one that has consistently chosen short-term power over long-term nation-building.

# The System That Serves Itself

The Constitution of India opens with three words that contain the entire philosophy of a functioning democracy: “We, the People.” Not “We, the Party.” Not “We, the Leader.” Every minister, every MP, every municipal councillor is a public servant, hired by the citizen, paid by the taxpayer, and answerable to the nation. That relationship has been completely inverted.

Parliament, once called the “temple of democracy” now sits for barely 70 days a year, compared to 120–140 days in the 1950s. Bills that govern hundreds of millions are passed in 6 minutes. Question Hour functioned at just 23% of scheduled time in the Lok Sabha and a shocking 6% in the Rajya Sabha during one recent session.

![](/assets/images/pic1.webp)

The Prime Minister of India has not held an unscripted press conference in over eleven years. The one time he appeared at a press conference in 2019, he let a colleague answer every question. He sat in silence. That is the closest the head of the world’s largest democracy has come to facing the press in more than a decade.

“When every question leads to a personal attack, a whataboutery spiral, a religious digression, a shouting match, or a procedural block, the citizen eventually concludes: ‘Nothing will change anyway. Why bother?’ That cynicism is the actual goal.”

The deflection playbook is consistent and predictable: attack the questioner rather than answer the question; invoke 1984 when asked about 2002; bring religion into any policy debate; shout until the time runs out; declare the question itself inadmissible. The most sophisticated aspect is not any single tactic, it is the cumulative effect of exhaustion. An angry citizen is dangerous. A cynical, disengaged citizen is manageable.

# The Corruption That Everyone Shares

India placed 91st in the 2025 Corruption Perceptions Index with a score of 39 out of 100, below the global average. But the more important and more suppressed truth is this: corruption in India is not a BJP problem, or a Congress problem. It is a political class problem.

The Electoral Bonds scandal, which the Supreme Court unanimously struck down as unconstitutional, exposed over $1.98 billion in opaque corporate-to-party funding. The BJP received approximately 55–60% of all bonds. Congress received 9–10%. The Trinamool Congress was the third largest beneficiary. The DMK received over ₹656 crore. Every major party had its hand in the jar and when the jar was broken open by the Supreme Court, every major party went quiet.

![](/assets/images/pic2.webp)

The PM CARES Fund, which collected thousands of crores from public sector employees and corporations during a national emergency, has never been audited by the Comptroller and Auditor General, has never been subject to RTI disclosure, and as recently as February 2026, the PMO directed that questions on PM CARES are inadmissible in Parliament. Public money, raised in the public’s name, answerable to no one.

# The Brainwash Industrial Complex

A democracy cannot be subverted by force alone. It must be subverted by consent and consent is manufactured. In India today, every medium that a citizen consumes has been converted into a propaganda delivery system with extraordinary efficiency.

Hindi cinema has veered sharply rightward. A string of blockbusters; The Kashmir Files, The Kerala Story, Uri, Bastar, Article 370, The Sabarmati Report form an assembly line of ideological content, each following the same template; BJP’s preferred villains (medieval Muslim rulers, Pakistan, Islamists, Congress) presented through one-sided narratives that align with Hindutva ideology.

The television news debate, an institution that should strengthen democracy, has been converted into its opposite. Ten panelists shout simultaneously, the anchor controls who speaks and when. Opposition voices are cut off mid-sentence. The same three BJP-friendly narratives repeat across every channel, every hour: Muslims are dangerous. Congress betrayed India. Modi is the only solution.

With 400 million Indians forming WhatsApp’s largest user base in the world, disinformation travels at the speed of a forward button. AI-generated deepfake videos went viral with half a million views before any fact-check reached anyone. By then, the damage was done.

And then there are the newspapers, their front pages colonised by government scheme advertisements designed to look like news stories, their editorial independence purchased by ₹1.95 crore per day in government advertising. In BJP-ruled states, teachers were given holidays to watch propaganda films free of cost. The school that was supposed to teach critical thinking becomes a recruitment office for ideology.

# The Schools Deliberately Closed

In the past ten years, approximately 89,441 government schools have been shut across India, 73% of them in BJP-ruled states. The government promised to spend 6% of GDP on education. The actual allocation for school education fell from 3.16% of the total budget in 2013–14 to 1.53% in 2024–25, nearly halved in a decade.

This is not administrative failure. It is policy direction. An educated, critically thinking population is the single greatest threat to any government that relies on manipulation for its mandate. Close the free schools and the poor cannot access education. Rewrite the textbooks and the students who remain learn a sanitized history. Shift University funding from grants to loans and independent academic thought becomes financially unviable.

NCERT textbooks have been altered to delete chapters on the RSS’s role in Gandhi’s assassination, references to the 2002 Gujarat pogrom, discussions of the Babri Masjid demolition, and significant portions of Mughal history. The brainwash and the defunding of education are not two separate policies. They are the same policy.

# Nationalism vs Patriotism: The Distinction That Changes Everything

At the heart of every failure documented in this article lies a single confusion that the political class has worked very hard to maintain, because their power depends on it. It is the confusion between nationalism and patriotism.

![](/assets/images/pic3.webp)

Bhagat Singh was 23 years old when he died for an India that did not yet exist and he questioned both British colonialism and Hindu communalism with equal ferocity. Ambedkar drafted a Constitution for a just India while being discriminated against by the very society he was serving. Tagore returned his knighthood not with rage, but with grief. These were patriots. They questioned. They built. They refused silence.

“Nationalism demands obedience. Patriotism demands accountability. A true patriot asks: ‘Is my government worthy of this nation?’ A nationalist asks: ‘How dare you question the government?’ India was built by the former. It is being consumed by the latter.”

The “anti-national” label, the most weaponised phrase in Indian political life now, has no legal definition. A parliamentary panel sought one from the Ministry of Information and Broadcasting in 2021 and received none. It exists purely as a political cudgel. When a Manipuri mother demanding justice is called anti-national, when a farmer protesting unpaid debts is called anti-national, when a journalist asking about PM CARES is called anti-national, the word has lost all meaning except one: silence.

# The Nation Not Built - Freebies Over Foundations

Unconditional cash transfers and freebie schemes across Indian states cost approximately ₹1.7 lakh crore in FY26 alone. That money could have built 170 AIIMS hospitals, funded India’s entire defense R&D for a decade, or created thousands of kilometers of metro rail. Instead it bought free rice, free electricity, and free gas cylinders; consumed, forgotten, and repeated every election cycle.

India has 65% youth population, the largest demographic dividend in human history. Yet 51% of young Indians are unemployable. Manufacturing as a share of GDP has declined to 13%. India imports 70% of its defence equipment. It has no domestic semiconductor fabrication at scale. It depends on China for solar panels while claiming green energy leadership.

The freebie model is not a welfare failure. It is a governance design choice. An educated, employed, self-sufficient citizen demands accountability. A hungry, unemployed, subsidy-dependent citizen demands continuation. Every rupee spent on free rice instead of a school is a vote purchased at the cost of a child’s future. Poverty is not India’s destiny. In too many cases, it is its political strategy.

# What India Actually Is - The Squandered Giant

None of what precedes this section changes the following fact: India is extraordinary. India has 42.4% of its population under 25, the largest youth dividend in human history, with a window that peaks around 2041 before beginning to decline. India remains the fastest growing major economy at 6.6% GDP growth. Services exports hit an all-time high of $387.5 billion in FY25. Indians account for 38% of doctors in the United States, 36% of scientists at NASA, 12% of scientists globally.

McKinsey identifies 18 future economic arenas; EVs, biopharma, aerospace, cloud services, advanced manufacturing, where India has genuine breakthrough potential. The talent exists. The ambition exists. The entrepreneurial instinct exists in extraordinary abundance.

What does not exist, yet, is the political class willing to invest in it rather than exploit it. Every engineer who leaves for Silicon Valley is not abandoning India. They are being abandoned by an India that built no space for them at home.

# The First Step Is Understanding

Every crisis documented in this article; the corruption, the propaganda, the nationalism, the freebies, the accountability vacuum, the closed schools, the performative parliament, flows from one foundational confusion. The confusion between loving your country and obeying your government. The confusion between Patriotism and Nationalism.

Fix that one understanding, and everything else becomes possible. Not immediately. Not easily. But possible. Because a citizen who understands the difference between nationalism and patriotism cannot be silenced with an “anti-national” slogan. Cannot be bought with a free cylinder. Cannot be distracted by a propaganda film. Cannot be satisfied by a speech with no follow-up.

They become, instead, exactly what the Constitution imagined: a citizen who holds power accountable not despite loving India, but because of it.

The patriots are not gone. They are grieving, frustrated, and drowned out by noise. They are the student who picks up litter and gets laughed at. The journalist who asks the real question and gets called anti-national. The engineer who wants to build in India but finds no opportunity. The voter who wants to choose accountability but finds no one to vote for.

They exist. And if you found yourself nodding through this article, they are you.
